Science-driven rewilding

Bringing wolves
back to Utah.

Gray wolves were extirpated from Utah by the 1930s. We use population genomics, habitat modeling, and open science to build the case for their return — and to ensure it succeeds.

About

Scientist. Builder.
Advocate for the wild.

I'm a bioinformatics student at Utah Valley University building the computational and scientific case for gray wolf reintroduction in Utah. My work sits at the intersection of conservation genomics, population biology, and open-source software.

Rewild Utah began as a personal mission and has grown into a structured research program. I develop publicly available tools for population genomic analysis, publish preprints on wolf population structure and reintroduction feasibility, and work to translate that science into accessible public education.

I founded Rewild Genomics LLC to house this work and pursue grant funding that can support wet-lab partnerships and expanded research.

Research

Current & planned work

All research is conducted using publicly available whole-genome sequencing data and open-source tools. Pipelines are published on GitHub and designed to be reproduced and extended.

CRISPR / Genomics

TMPRSS2 gRNA design for CDV-resistant wolves

649 candidate gRNAs scanned, top 5 BLAST-validated against the wolf genome. Rank #1 candidate scores 0.930 with zero off-target hits. Targeting the CDV entry protease in Canis lupus baileyi.

Complete — preprint in prep View analysis →

Population genomics

Mitochondrial diversity across global wolf populations

100 complete mitochondrial genomes across 12 populations. 14× diversity gap between Iberian and Mexican wolves. NJ phylogeny confirms North American wolves as the appropriate Utah reintroduction lineage.

Complete — preprint in prep View analysis →

Population genomics

Wolf source population analysis for Utah reintroduction

Whole-genome PCA, ADMIXTURE modeling, FST, and runs-of-homozygosity inbreeding analysis across Rocky Mountain wolf populations to identify optimal founder stock.

In progress

Bioinformatics tools

Open-source wolf population genomics pipeline

Snakemake pipeline wrapping GATK, PLINK, ADMIXTURE, and VCFtools for end-to-end population genomic analysis. Designed for conservation researchers without HPC access.

Habitat modeling

Utah wolf habitat suitability & prey base assessment

Species distribution modeling integrating GBIF occurrence data, ungulate biomass estimates, terrain, and human conflict risk to produce spatially explicit habitat maps.

Policy analysis

Regulatory landscape for Utah wolf reintroduction

Review of ESA delisting history, Utah state law, tribal consultation requirements, and pathways for formal reintroduction under Section 10(j) experimental populations.

Ancient DNA

Historical wolf genetic diversity in the Great Basin
